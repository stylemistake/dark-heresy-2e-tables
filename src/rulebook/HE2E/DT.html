122
123
INITIAL SURPRISEBACK TO TOPINITIAL SURPRISE EFFECTBACK TO TOPREMAINING CONCEALEDBACK TO TOP
124
At the beginning of a combat, the GM determines whether any characters are Surprised. This may happen because of an ambush, when being attacked while sleeping, or due to a very unexpected backstab. Surprise is never triggered by simply "choosing to act first", and some special circumstances must be present - such as kicking the door out before a raid. There will be many combats wherein nobody is Surprised, usually many more than those where someone is.Surprised targets remain still and dumbfounded while the enemy drops on them. Any surprised targets skip their first turn, and they cannot dodge, parry, or take any evasive actions. Any team members of the assailants that have stayed away for the ambush also lose their first turn, and by design still remain undetected until they enter combat (if they were detected, that means they were not far away). This also applies to any other entities that are further away.
During this first turn, assailants receive a +30 bonus to BS/WS tests on Surprised characters.
Note that some opponents may evade Surprise, and could potentially act normally on the first turn. They may even act before the assailants! On the other hand, if the dice fall just right, it is also possible for the assailants to have two turns in a row.		Under usual circumstances, the attacker and any of his nearby allies are automatically revealed after the first attack. The GM may decide that this is not the case in some of the more enigmatic cases - such as sniping from a rooftop a several hundred metres away, or spitting a silent poisonous dart from a bush. In these cases, instead of being automatically revealed, the attacker is subjected to a collective Awareness test to attempt and find him. This test can range from a Challenging(+0) test to discern a gunner armed with a small pistol 50m away in a very dense and loud crowd, to a Hellish (-60) test to see a disguised sniper shooting with a silencer from one of the skyscrapers of a hive, 700m away. The point is, that not getting noticed after shooting is hard.
If you possess the Stealth skill, you may dedicate a Half-Action to seek cover immediately after shooting. If you manage to slip back into cover by using this skill, the difficulty to notice your exact location goes up to Challenging (+0). This effectively shortens the safe distance for an ambush, because a shooter as close as 20m away can attempt this and then have the chance to escape relatively undetected.
Even if your exact location was not detected, enemies are still made aware to a 90 degree arc in which you may reside.

PREPARING AN AMBUSH / BACKSTABBACK TO TOP
134
An ambush can be quite easily prepared by hiding in a bush, or wearing an undistinguishable hood in a crowd. However, this kind of ambush is quite sloppily done, and does not guarantee success, as targets can simply test awareness/scrutiny to avoid it.
An ambush has a much bigger chance of happening when using the Deceive or Stealth skills, when appropriate. Successful use of this skill makes Surprise automatic against unprepared targets, and increases its chances against alerted enemies.
When executing a surprise move, it may be that some characters are better hidden than others, and one rotten apple can break the game for everyone. Whenever a group of characters are waiting in ambush, awareness is only tested once to detect them all. The character that affords the worst chances to remain hidden is tested against. If this character is detected, then the whole group is discovered, and the ambush fails.
If some characters do not want to spoil the ambush for his friends, he may retreat further from the ambush location, and away from the targets. This allows his friends better chances to succeed on the ambush; however, he will then find himself quite far away once extended fighting begins. The safe distance varies depending on the surroundings, and ultimately is decided by the GM. As an example, it may be as close as 20 metres in the foliage of a jungle, and as far as 200 metres in rocky grasslands.

 If the assassin has not taken extra effort to conceal his position (using Stealth and Deceive skills), Surprised targets should usually be able to test Awareness before the ambush, or Scrutiny before a backstab. If they are successful, they are not surprised. Every party member rolls separately to avoid this.
In some cases, it is possible to react well to even a well-planned ambush. Usually this involves drawing your weapon and slowly advancing forward, akin to a Half-Move and an Overwatch. Or in case of a suspcious back-stabby situation, drawing your weapon and keeping your eye on the suspect. In narrative time, this translates to Half moving speed, and does not actually grant any combat moves.
If this carefull approach is taken, surprise candidates can test awareness / scrutiny even if a Deceive/Stealth skill was successfuly passed beforehand. This makes the awareness/scrutiny test an opposed test. Use only 1 awareness or scrutiny roll, and compare it to all applicable assailants.

CAUSING PINNINGBACK TO TOPSHOOTING INTO MELEEBACK TO TOPWEAPON JAMSBACK TO TOP
209
Being shot at is a terrifying experience at the best of times and even the most inexperienced Acolytes know to keep their heads down when the bullets and bolt shells start flying. Pinning represents a character’s survival instincts telling him to stay in cover. While he may want to charge headlong into a storm of bullets, he first needs to steel his nerves. A Pinning test is a Willpower Test, failing which results in the character being Pinned. Pinning tests are required when:
- Every time when being shot at by Full-Auto fire. These tests are Easy(+20). Full-Auto overwatch shots also trigger this test.
- Every time when either standing in or moving into a Suppression zone. These tests are Challenging(+0)
- Every second time when getting shot by Semi-Auto fire. The first and every other semi-auto attack does not cause pinning. This counter is reset every turn, so getting shot at by a single Semi-Auto fire each turn will never cause pinning. These tests are Challenging(+0)
- In case of shooting into melee, all participants are subject to the Pinning test.		 There will be times when a target is already engaged with another enemy. You can still make a ranged attack at an opponent engaged in melee, but you may not hit what you want.Such is the capricious whim of the 41st Millennium that many of the ranged weapons that an Acolyte uses will have an unfortunate tendency to malfunction, either through extreme age, maltreatment of their machine spirit, or just plain poor design. To represent these unfortunate occurrences, an unmodified result of 96 to 00, in addition to being an automatic miss, also indicates the weapon has jammed. A Jammed weapon cannot be fired until it is cleared. Clearing a Jam is a Full Action which requires a Ballistic Skill Test. If the Test is successful then the Jam has been cleared, though the weapon needs to be reloaded and any ammo in it is lost. If the Test is failed the weapon is still Jammed, though the character can attempt to clear it again next Round.
Note: Some weapons, such as plasma guns, grenades and missiles, are particularly dangerous to use. For these weapons refer to their descriptions and Weapon Special Qualities (see Chapter V: Armoury). Semi-automatic and fully automatic fire also increases the likelihood of Jamming. This is described within the Semi-Auto Burst, Full Auto Burst and Suppressing Fire Actions.

Pinning lasts until the end of the next turn of the pinned character. If the character is pinned during his own turn (for example, by running into overwatch), pinning lasts until the end of his next turn. A Pinned character suffers these penalties:
• -20 Penalty to BS, Int, WP and Fel tests. This is not applied if the pinned character was not hit by the attack that pinned him and if all of his body parts are completely behind cover relative to the attack (both conditions must be met to avoid pinning).

BACK TO TOP
260
There are bound to be times when you want to climb over a wall, scale a cliff, or scramble up a tree to escape the claws of a vicious xenos. Any character with both hands free can climb any non-sheer surface with a Strength (or Climb) Test. On a success, you ascend or descend at a rate of one-half your Half Move speed (to a minimum of a 1/2 metre). For each degree of success, you climb an extra metre. On a failed Test, you fall from your starting climbing position. Climbing Difficulty varies with the nature of the climbing surface, though most tests should be Challenging (+0).At some time a character is bound to find himself involved in a chase, pursuing or being pursued by unfriendly heretics. This can be a thrilling component to roleplaying games, especially those involving dangerous environments, but such encounters can be a chore to run. In addition, rigid rules of HE2E may lead to boringly predictable results, such as a chracter with +1AgB always escaping on ever ground with no deviance, ever. To resolve pursuit simply, use the following rules.

Engaging a Pursuit
A fleeing character engages pursuit whenever he finishes his/her turn further from his enemies than could be reached by a standart run action by any of them. If you are still surrounded by enemies from multiple sides, you may not engage pursuit even if you have enough distance to your enemies - you must slip through a window between them. In short, pursuit rules are not fit if you are running towards your enemies. GM chooses whether to engage pursuit or not, not the player.
When Pursuit is engaged, the escaping character immediately gains 3 Pursuit points, and all of his present opponents gain 2 Pursuit points. GM may rule that more distant opponents (>2 turns of running distance away from the spot where the escapees begun running) only gain 1 Pursuit Point, and very distant opponents gain none at all.

Sheer Surfaces
Certain surfaces are beyond the means of ordinary characters to climb. Naked stone walls without hand-holds, vertical ascents and overhangs all require some skill at climbing to navigate. You may attempt a Climb Skill Test to climb these surfaces at one-half your Half Move speed. Otherwise, you must have tools such as a knotted rope or blanket, rope ladder or ladder. If you can take your time and are not threatened by opponents, you may ascend at one-half your Half Move rate without Testing Strength. You may try to climb more quickly or when you are being attacked, but you must Test Strength as if you were climbing a non-sheer surface.

A Pursuit Dissected
All pursuits occur in Detailed Time, so participants move in the same order as during combat. If there was no combat, roll Initiative.
On their Turn, the character can Chase or Flee by making an Agility Test. The GM assigns the Difficulty based on the environment, with open and clear areas being Easy (+30), sparse streets being Challenging (+0) , and a densely packed marketplace would be Very Hard (–30). Alternatively, any present character may take normal Actions (such as shooting at the fleeing opponent or pursuer, using a power, and so on), but in this case they lose 1 Pursuit point. Keep in mind that normal distance is covered during pursuit, so it is up to GM to say how hard is your target to hit. Any characters that ignore the escapee and possibly continue local combat, also lose 1 Pursuit point.
A success on the Chase/Flee agility test equals 1 Pursuit Point plus 1 Pursuit Point for each degree of success. A failure does not provide any Pursuit points, and even reduces the number of Pursuit Points by 1 for every DoF.

A character with negative Pursuit Points loses the tracks and is effectively out of the chase or, for the fleeing character, pulled out of the chase.
