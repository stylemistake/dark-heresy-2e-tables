What are these rules?
 Here you will find the rules to use when creating your character. GM should oversee this process heavily, and he always gets the final say on what can or cannot be used. After all, GM will be providing a framework in which action will take place, so he has to make sure that all of the created characters make sense in the end.
While the GM can override everything and distribute whatever characters he likes, we suggest using a semi-randomized process to generate more interesting characters. If GM decides to use this system, please proceed. Please note that GM can both allow more choices in the system, and he can restrict almost everything.		1.       Homeworld. Roll characteristics and age. Apply homeworld bonus. Roll wounds and Fate points.

STEP 1: HOMEWORLD
46
Homeworld establishes the character's place of origin and their childhood. It leaves a significant impact on the person, determines which characteristics were honed in the character's surroundings, and describes what paths were open to them once they matured. The selection is not limited to homeworlds - as the same planet could house a highborn, a hiver, a ratling, and an underhiver, which would all begin their life with a different perspective.
47
48
Feral worldThe populations of feral worlds have long ago regressed to primal savagery, often over very long periods of isolation. The technological level of these worlds remains pre-black powder or even Stone Age. Often the population consists of hunter-gatherer societies using primitive tools and weapons, and in almost all cases human culture is based around the interactions of various tribes, gangs or creeds.
49
Forge worldForge Worlds are the worlds of the Adeptus Mechanicus - worlds totally devoted to industry and to the Cult Mechanicus's own occult worship. The majority of a forge-world's solid ground surface is covered in thousands of years of built-up, massive industrial complexes: great machine factories, production centres, huge volcanic furnaces, skyscraping chimneys, abyss-like quarries and the great workshop-fortresses of the Collegia Titanica. No other world can match the quality and quantity of a forge world's output.
50
HighbornHighborn are the elite of Imperium: the nobles, princes, and lords of cities, systems, and worlds. To be born into such a setting is to have been given the best the planet has to offer, raised apart from the struggling ranks of Mankind, and destined to take on positions of great import and power. To be a highborn is also to enter into a world of deadly politics and ancient feuds, where children grow up with terrible enemies and sleep knowing there are those that would cut their throats for a taste of their hereditary position.
51
HiveHives consist of great housing and factory blocks, forming ranges of man-made mountains. These huge towering urban complexes are known as city hives, or simply as hives, and their individual peaks or towers are called city spires or spires. A Hive consists of many layers; the lowest layers form the underhive. Hives are worlds in themselves, where the populace has never seen the outer world, and concepts such as the ground and sky are completely alien. There are entire regions inside the hive blocked off by collapsed sections, where is found the treasure of the hive, the Archeotech hoards, ancient stores of technology.
52
UnderhiveUnderhive is the lowest layer of a hive. It is the most run-down area where most of the rules are created by gangs. It is a difficult place to live, where the power has long been turned off, and where most sewage and junk pipelines end. People rule through fear and shun the rich above them in the better apartments.
53
Shrine worldShrine worlders grow up on worlds that the Imperium has deemed holy places, where the Cult of the Emperor’s power is omnipresent. While their birthplace might range from cluttered, decaying cities to scattered farmlands, or their planet from a green paradise to a forbidding rock, the power of the Emperor saturates it. Religious grace permeates every part of a shrine world, the very spirit of the Cult of the Imperial Creed embodied by the world itself and its citizens, who embrace His divine worship.
54
VoidbornVoidborn spend their lives in the expanses of space, growing up on ancient orbital stations, generation vessels, merchant freighters, or remote asteroid outposts. They spend much of their lives without the stability of a real world beneath their feet, knowing the cold, deadly grasp of empty space is only a few metres of bulkhead away. There is something deeply odd about the voidborn, something intangible that lurks below the surface, writhing in the dark like the Warp lurks below space itself, waiting and watching for its moment to surface.
55
Garden worldA paradise world (also known as a garden world or pleasure world) is a class of planet characterized by its outstanding natural beauty. Its natural conditions are perfect for human habitation. They have little, if any, industry and low populations. Instead of populating or industrializing these rare planets, some are retained in their natural state and used as recreational bases for important Imperial servants. On these planets, warriors may train their bodies and minds for war, studying arcane battle-philosophy and practising martial arts.
56
Death worldDeath World is a planet in which the native flora and fauna has evolved into naturally aggressive and dangerous forms. These eco-systems are finely balanced between continual destruction and lightning-fast reproduction. Death worlds take many forms, ranging from jungle-covered hell-holes with carnivorous plants and animals to barren, volcanic wastelands racked by ion storms. Humans can, and do, live on these worlds, but it is a never-ending struggle.
57
Daemon worldA daemon world is a planet twisted and corrupted by the power of Chaos and the warp from being trapped in a prolonged warp rift. Entwining the impossibilities of the warp and the reality of the physical realm, Daemon Worlds are not bound by the laws of physics, but by the whim of daemonic rulers and the emotions of mortals. Those few Death worlds within the borders of the Imperium are rigorously guarded by the Inquisition, since, unfortunately, because of the reality-shifting effects of the Warp, it is all but impossible to subject them to Exterminatus
58
Quarantine worldQuarantined Worlds are an Imperial classification of planet. These worlds are too dangerous to settle or explore, but still seen as too valuable to destroy or ignore completely. These planets are placed under strict quarantine by the Administratum or Inquisition and sometimes are even blockaded to ensure that nothing may enter or leave its surface until the Imperium can properly utilize them.
59
Agriculture worldAn Agri-World is one devoted to food production. The majority of its surface is given over to producing food for other worlds reliant on such imports - the food itself forms part of the planet's required tithe. Governors of such planets are required by the Adeptus Terra to protect the harvest and meet the quotas places on them. Often inter-commander rivalry leads to attempts to destroy or steal crops and livestock and then blaming them on pirates or raids.
60
Feudal worldA feudal world is a classification of world existing in a technologically medieval state. The most advanced such worlds possess black powder weaponry. Feudal worlds are self-sufficient, and due to dependence on agriculture, a large part of the populations of these worlds are peasants, usually ruled by warrior aristocracies. Feudal worlds are of relatively little use to the Imperium; the true position of their place in the universe may constitute something of a culture shock to the inhabitants, making them poor material for Imperial service - although the warrior nobilities are often a source of recruits for the Space Marines.
61
Frontier worldFrontier Worlds are recently settled worlds which may not be totally explored or have a fully established government in place. Often they are havens for those escaping the harsh rule of the Imperium, or more often escaping the judgement of the Imperium, and as such often have a lawless reputation.
62
RatlingRace: Ratlings are the smallest type of abhuman, their ancestors having become stunted by thousands of years of inbreeding on worlds with naturally soporific climates and abundant harvests. Ratling home worlds are self-sufficient and agricultural (often - Agri Worlds or Feudal Worlds), and the hedonistic and unambitious nature of Ratlings means that these worlds do not progress beyond a medieval level of civilisation.
63
OgrynRace: Ogryns (Homo Sapiens Gigantus) are the largest and most physically powerful type of abhuman. Ogryns evolved on worlds with harsh and barren environments and high gravity. Most of these worlds, having no other use to humanity, were originally used as prison planets. They make ideal warriors and are often recruited into Imperial Guard regiments, and used as close assault shock troops.

STEP 3: BACKGROUND
96
While home worlds establish a place of origin, backgrounds indicate an Acolyte’s previous life experience before an Inquisitor noticed the aptitudes and abilities he developed there, found them to be of use, and forever changed the Acolyte’s destiny. However the acolyte came into this background is not strictly defined and may be left to the story - either it was pure skill, selection luck, or simply family.
97
98
Adeptus AdministratumAdministratum is the backbone of Imperial administration and biurocracy. They work on all levels of Imperial society and processes such enormous quantities of information, that those within its ranks can sometimes gain understanding of the workings of the Imperium unlike any other.
In Inquisition: Administratum officials are usually chosen either for their ability to bend the system, or for the vast knowledge and understanding that they can provide for teams. These characters can provide huge strategic and supply support for the team, and make ideal back-room schemers, advisors and strategists.
99
Adeptus ArbitesArbites are the vigil enforcers of brutal laws, without which Imperium would not survive. Arbites care not for petty crimes. Instead, Arbites take on corrupted and ineffectual Imperial organizations, forcing them back into function by a wave o investigations, interrogations, purges, and executions. Arbites answer only to Judges; not to local authorities.
In Inquisition: Arbites are usually recruited for their investigative skills. They make great trackers, detectives and interrogators - although it's not quite subtle. In addition, they can hold their own in a battle. Finally, arbitrators keep some of their contacts back among arbitrators - which may help to get supplies and help, but may also influence the arbiter himself.
100
Adeptus Astra TelepathicaThe Adeptus Astra Telepathica oversees psykers within the Imperium, scouring the galaxy for new psykers and then examining and training their catches. Many of the new cadre are sacrificed to sustain the Emperor, but some remain as Black Ship crew, advisors, or military specialists.
In Inquisition: Adeptus Astra Telepathica characters are recruited for their experience with psychic phenomena. Most of them are psykers, and provide their fair shair with their powers. Others, however, are non-gifted people, who have displayed an unusual aptitude to resist and battle psykers. Either way, these characters are heavily specialised towards the mental arts.
101
Adeptus MechanicusThe Adeptus Mechanicus are the custodians of all Imperial technology. But they are not inventors, and usually they focus on preserving and replicating. The ultimate goal of a mechanicus character is to become more like the divine machine god - complete with metal skin and iron heart.
In Inquisition: Mechanicus provides their benefit with techical expertise and support. They can repair and construct almost anything, and sometimes they can even hack. But their focus on preservation does not allow them to cause much harm to machines, or to improvise. Still, if softer approach fails, the enemies of a tech-priest may find themselves facing a living metal turret.
102
Adeptus MinistorumAdeptus Ministorum, or so called Ecclesiarchy, is dedicated to worshiping the Emperor and preaching the divine right of Mankind to rule the stars. Viewing themselves as holding Imperium together, they are the bonding missionaries and priests to the faithful, and the holy executors to the heretical and daemonic.
In Inquisition: Ecclesiarchy are usually invited for their mediation abilities and their effect on troop morale. During investigations, they can be extremely persuasive. During combat, they bring along an aura of faith that can turn a hopeless rout into a decisive victory. While most choose a rather passive approach, others inspire faith with a flamer and a sword.
103
Imperial GuardThe Astra Militarum is the largest coherent fighting force in the galaxy. They are comprised of countless billions of men and women, supported by heavy armor. They are usually the first Imperial force to respond to a threat if a planet's Planetary Defence Force fails to suppress it.
In Inquisition: Guardsmen come into Inquisition almost uniformly for their craft of war, although a few also draw attention for their leadership abilities. They shine in battle. Although most usually enjoy the role of an assault trooper, guardsmen are also exceptionally trained in survival and reconaissance.
104
Imperial NavyThe Imperial Navy are responsable for the fleets of starships that maintain order between the stars and planets in the Imperium. They also provide air support and transport to the Guardsman legions. Although both organizations are to defend mankind, they are deliberately kept separate so that the military does not grow too powerful.
In Inquisition: Good navy crew are valued as all-rounded military specialists. While they are nowhere close in understanding as Tech-priests, they have had just enough practice to keep an old engine running, and track where to steer the wheel at the same time. The true advantage of crewmen comes in space, where they have spent countles years.
105
OutcastOutcasts do not belong to any of the greater organizations of the Imperium. They are the hardy people who plow the fields, work in factories, and trade petty things. Each of them follows their own lonely Emperor's plan. Many are born and die in the same village, never knowing much about the worlds beyond their own.
In Inquisition: It takes exceptional deeds for a commoner to be noticed by the Inquisition. It is even rarer for these deeds to be noble. Quite often they are gang backstabbers, who saved their lives by squeeling about something big. At other times they are mercenaries, notorious for their ability to spill blood. Either way, they were deemed pure and skillful enough.

STEP 4: ROLE
145
Deciding on a character’s role is perhaps the single most important stage of character creation for the player, for it determines what type of character he brings to the team. Home world and background contribute a great deal to the character’s history before he became who he is, but his role sets out his days to come and how he operates in his team.

 Human psykers are collected by the millions each day across the galaxy and brought to Terra on the dread Black Ships of the Adeptus Astra Telepathica, as part of the binding tithes each world owes to the Imperium. Here they are tested for power levels, control, strength of will, and other factors to decide their fate. Once this sanctioning process is over, a psyker goes on to serve the Imperium either in life or death. All psykers are viewed with suspicion at best, and outright hatred at worst, and even sanctioned psykers are always suspect. Those psykers who never underwent this testing and training to help ensure the safety of those around them are deemed rogue, fugitives from Imperial law should they be discovered.
These psykers can represent anyone from a heretical magus who delves far into the Warp in search of greater power, to a fringe world shaman casting bones to divine the future, to a once-good man driven to foul deeds merely to survive, to an Acolyte using his powers under the aegis of his Inquisitor’s authority. A rogue might even be an Inquisitor himself who discovered his powers late in life and never submitted to the testing of the Adeptus Astra Telepathica. The only thing they share is that they were never taken aboard the Black Ships. Most end their short days atop a fiery pyre or as a Daemon’s plaything, but some become mighty forces for damnation or salvation.
