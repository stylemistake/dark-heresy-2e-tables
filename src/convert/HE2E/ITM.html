BIONICS IN GENERAL
209
Bionic replacement limbs are assumed to operate at the same level of strength and dexterity as the body they are attached to (rather than risk ripping themselves out of their host through overpressure). Replacement and additional limbs (such as mecha-endrites) can only be used to perform tasks that the owner already knows how to do. So for example, a character with a medicae mechadendrite must have the Medicae skill in order to take advantage of the extra abilities the limb grants.
• Note that bonuses and penalties relate only to tests made using the bionic limb, and characters that have two bionic limbs do not gain double the bonus, but rather they may apply the bonus regardless of which bionic limb they are using.
• All Bionics are only available in Normal quality, unless otherwise noted.
• Some Bionics require a Mechadendrite Use (Weapon or Utility) talent from the Tech ability tree. Without it, it is useless. If the trait is required, the class (Weapon, Utility) is noted next to the implant.

INSTALLING BIONICS
211
Gaining the Bionic does not install it, except when they are requested at the start of the game. Instead, the character has to find a suitable specialist who would perform the surgery. The test to install a bionic is a Medicae test, and has a difficulty equal to the base availability of the bionic. The performer of the surgery and anyone assisting must have both the Tech-Use and Medicae Skills. Not having one of these provides a -30 penalty, and not having both will kill the patient. If the surgeon is differently skilled in either Medicae or Tech-use, he uses the lesser bonus.
Success: the implant is successfuly installed. Patient gains 1d10 Rending wounds damage and temporarily loses 2d10 Toughness. He may not be treated with First aid until the next day.
Failure: the implant is not successfuly installed, but remains intact. Patient gains 2d10 Rending wounds damage, reduced by Toughness, and temporarily loses 2d10 Toughness. He may not be treated with First aid until the next day.
Failure by 2DoF: the implant is destroyed and lost. Patient gains 2d10 Rending wounds damage, reduced by Toughness, and temporarily loses 3d10 Toughness. He may not be treated with First aid until the next day.
Failure by 4DoF: the implant is destroyed and lost. Patient gains 3d10 Rending wounds damage, reduced by Toughness, and temporarily loses 4d10 Toughness. He may not be treated with First aid until the next day.

EFFECTS OF BIONICS
213
There are two kinds of bionics: Implants and Augmentations.
Augmentations have a rather small effect on the remaining body, and so do not provide additional effects. They do not count as implants and cannot be disabled as easily.
Implants, through their robus construction, add +2 to the owner's Armour against hits scored to the particular location where the implant is installed. However, any result that causes bleeding or some other inappropriate result renders the bionic limb useless. Terminal Damage to a limb that results in death has the full effect as it can be assumed that the limb explodes into shrapnel, incinerates or discharges stored energy through its owner with lethal effect. Implants enable the Flesh is Weak trait to those having Mechanicus Implants, although you have to have unique implants to level it up (ex. double arms will only count as one).
